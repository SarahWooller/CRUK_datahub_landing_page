Summary

The Kidney Cancer Longitudinal Dataset is a rich, integrated resource focused on understanding the etiology, progression, and treatment response of various kidney cancers, primarily Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC) subtypes. It follows patients over time, linking diverse data streams to provide a holistic view of the disease journey.

Key Data Components.

The CRCC includes 5,500 patient records and is structured into five core, linked domains:

1. Patient Demographics & Background Environment

This domain captures non-clinical factors potentially influencing cancer risk and outcomes.

  • Demographics: Age at diagnosis, sex, ethnicity, geographic location (initial residence).
  • Environmental Data: Detailed residential and occupational history (e.g., exposure to known carcinogens, industrial pollutants, heavy metals), lifestyle factors (smoking status, alcohol consumption, physical activity, diet).
  • Comorbidities: Pre-existing conditions (e.g., hypertension, diabetes, obesity) and family history of cancer.

2. Clinical and Treatment Data

This longitudinal component tracks the patient's medical course from initial presentation through follow-up.

  • Diagnosis and Staging: Cancer type and subtype (e.g., clear cell, papillary), tumor size, TNM staging (at diagnosis and recurrence), grade.
  • Medical Protocols and Treatments: Detailed records of all interventions:
  • Surgery: Type of nephrectomy (partial vs. radical), lymphadenectomy, date of procedure.
  • Systemic Therapy: Specific drug regimens, including targeted therapies (e.g., tyrosine kinase inhibitors), immunotherapy (e.g., checkpoint inhibitors), and chemotherapy; dosage, duration, and dates.
  • Radiation Therapy: Dosing, fractionation, and targets.
  • Outcomes: Disease-Free Survival (DFS), Overall Survival (OS), site and date of recurrence, treatment response criteria (e.g., RECIST scores).
